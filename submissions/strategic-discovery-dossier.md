# Problem summary

## What problem is Legacy-Trust trying to solve?

Agent's workflows are disorganised and untracked. They spend too much time checking 
whether someone else already contacted a customer, reconciling status across multiple 
files, and manually tracking promises to pay using spreadsheets and notes are out 
of sync. Gareth also hints at the fact that there is no clear beginning or end 
to their workflows.

## Why is it failing at scale?

The consequent delayed actions are contributing to a 15% revenue loss which is 
exacerbated by inconsistent follow-ups that likely result from the splintered and 
out-of-sync data. Customers are also feeling the impacts of this because they often 
repeat information because different records show different last-contact 
details - this will be a strong driver in revenue loss and churn rate.

## Evidence expected before phase 1 and why it matters financially/operationally?

For each automation process, we need measurable collections performance improvements, 
which will reduce the time agents spend on admin in the short term, and reduce 
operational costs in the long term through a more accessible and centralised collections 
system. Additionally, we need their associated savings, and the assumptions that 
will require trust from leadership. This must all be packaged in a way that is 
fully documented and traceable.
